Interesting Facts:

  • F1 cars are among the fastest racing cars globally, reaching speeds of over 230 mph (370 km/h) on some tracks.
  • F1 drivers experience high gravitational forces during races, especially during braking and cornering. They can experience forces up to 5G, meaning they feel five times their body weight.
  • Some F1 teams have been competing since the inception of the championship in 1950, such as Ferrari and McLaren.
  • F1 cars are designed with intricate aerodynamics to maximize downforce, ensuring better grip and stability at high speeds and through corners
  • F1 engines are highly efficient, extracting a remarkable amount of power from a relatively small amount of fuel.
